Introduction

Inkblot is one of the four currently available Eradicators in Shifted in Monochrome, representing Inkblot (the universe). They've had quite the history with Gray, but is merely a myth to most other denizens of their realm.
Though they may not be as powerful as in their own realm, their strength and lingering power leaves them a still-formidable foe towards Experiments.

Appearance

Inkblot is a tall, anthropomorphic feline-like creature with jet black fur covering their entire body. They have several mouths in various places on their body, though lacks one on their face. They have two glowing white eyes and similarly glowing white paw beans. While difficult to differentiate from the rest of their body, the fur around their paws and footpaws is raised partially.

During "Last Man Standing", they enter their Monarch form, their appearance altering slightly. The raised fur on their paws and footpaws become a glowing white color, while their paw beans turn jet black. Additionally four disembodied paws float behind them, also glowing white with black beans. On their back is a glowing mark shaped like the Greek letter "Psi (Ψ)".

Personality

In canon, they don't often show much in terms of personality, instead serving as more of a silent observer and guardian. They're capable of taking care of individuals who directly threaten the balance of their realm, and do so emotionlessly, but this is reserved for immensely dangerous individuals.

In Shifted in Monochrome, their personality is largely the same, but with a hint of reluctance. Their dialogue when killing an Experiment suggests that they'd rather not, but don't have a choice in the matter.

Gameplay

(This is all hypothetical because there isn't exactly any way of knowing how they play since there isn't really any gameplay)

Inkblot is an Eradicator who rewards positioning and stealth. Their abilities and decent damage allow them to quietly ambush Experiments, however their slower speed can make it difficult for players who prefer to rush into a group to take them out.

Abilities

Stalking Hunter & Inky Physiology

Keybind: N/A (Passive Abilities)
Cooldown: N/A (Passive Abilities)

Stalking Hunter: Inkblot has no audible footsteps until within 18 studs of an experiment. Additionally, they are unable to be properly viewed through digital means like Suvo's scope. They instead appear as a distortion.

Inky Physiology: Inkblot is stunned for longer through fire-based attacks or hazards, though is stunned for less time from ice-based attacks or hazards.

Heavy Hitter

Keybind: M1
Cooldown: 0.5s (First two hits), 1.5 (Final hit)

Inkblot does a three-hit attack, dealing 15 damage with the first two hits and 30 damage in the final hit.

Puddle

Keybind: E
Cooldown: 20s

Inkblot melts into a puddle, moderately increasing their speed and significantly increaasing their jump height. The form lasts for a maximum of 15 seconds and a minimum of 1 second. Using any other ability while in puddle form or letting the timer run out reverts Inkblot back to their normal form.

Snare

Keybind: Q
Cooldown: 25s

Inkblot throws a blob of ink, with the distance being dependent on how long the ability is charged for. If simply tapped, Inkblot will drop it at their feet. If held for 4 seconds, they will throw it 8 studs away. Upon contact with the floor, it spreads into a medium-sized yet thick puddle of ink that will trap Experiments for 5 seconds if they come into contact with it. If the blob comes into direct contact with an Experiment, they'll be trapped for 10 seconds instead. Experiments are able to mash their jump button to escape faster, and any attack that would stun an Eradicator will destroy the snare as well.

Omniscience

Keybind: X
Cooldown: 30s

All Experiments within a 50 stud radius are marked with a white highlight by Inkblot for 10 seconds.

Lore

Canon Lore

"Back in the beginning, it was just a sea of thick darkness—not too dissimilar to the Abyss you've no doubt heard of. Two beings, Terra and Inkblot, floated in the center of it. The two of them were bored and tired of the complete lack of anything, and conversed about giving meaning to it. The two of them put their powers together and created two realms named after them."

Having grown quite attached to their realm, Inkblot chose to be its primary guardian rather than create thralls to uphold the world's peace like Terra did. They would watch over it and take care of it, removing any potential threats to its harmony. Some people even claim to have seen a dark figure with piercing eyes watching them from the shadows, no doubt Inkblot observing their world.

But of course, those're all just stories passed down through the generations. It's nice to have some higher being to believe in, but I doubt they're actually real."

— An unknown writer.

Shifted in Monochrome Lore

"The world was quiet, just as it was most other days. Nothing notable was happening to cause it concern, yet it was watching everything regardless. Suddenly, it felt a disturbance. Something new. It manifested a physical form near the disturbance to observe it. It looked like an odd ring of pure white flame, similar yet dissimilar to the portals connecting the world and Terra. As the world observed the ring, it begin to pull, rising in intensity until the world couldn't fight against it anymore.

They awoke in a world unlike their own. A quiet voice gnawed in the back of their mind, urging for them to eradicate all of the experiments. Maybe then they'd be able to return to their own realm."

Trivia

Note: Inkblot (the character) will be referred to as Inkblot-A to avoid confusion with Inkblot (the universe). The universe will just be referred to as Inkblot.

  • During the very early concepting phase for Shifted in Monochrome (prior to deciding to make it an actual game) one of Gray's EXEs, Beast, was originally planned to represent Inkblot as a killer.
  • Back before Gray had played Outcome Memories, they assumed Shifted in Monochrome would be more similar to Dead by Daylight, leading to them making 3 separate "Terror Radii" tracks.
    • In a similar music-related mishap, Inkblot-A's "Last Life Chase" initially was meant to be an LMS theme before Gray was informed that LMS themes were based on the Experiment and not the Eradicator.
  • Though their height was normalized for gameplay purposes, Inkblot-A is 9'3" (about 282cm)
  • Inkblot-A has the Greek letter psi as a symbol on their back due to it being used as a general symbol to denote Inkblot-related content.
