Introduction

Metal Dankles is one of the four currently available Eradicators in Shifted in Monochrome, representing the Coryverse.

Appearance

Metal Dankles looks nearly identical to Metal Knuckles from the Sonic the Hedgehog franchise, however wears pixelated sunglasses with a white shine.

Abilities

Dead or Alive & Flight

Keybind: N/A (Passive Abilities)
Cooldown: N/A (Passive Abilities)

Dead or Alive: Metal Dankles has a rare chance to land a critical hit when attacking Dankles or Snow & Tempo, applying additional damage to them. Dankles takes more damage from the critical hit.

Flight: Metal Dankles can fly upon pressing the jump button twice. He remains able to attack while flying.

Slash

Keybind: M1
Cooldown: TBD (Short)

Metal Dankles attacks using their claws. The ability does little damage to an Experiment, however has a fast cooldown.

Icicle Crash

Keybind: E
Cooldown: TBD

Metal Dankles stops in place and charges up, an icy aura forming around him as he does. Once fully charged, he flies forwards at a considerable speed, dealing damage to any survivor caught in his path.

If used while flying, Metal Dankles instead dives straight down into the ground, emitting a large, icy shockwave that has a chance to inflict Frostbite on any Experiments caught up in it.

Ice Spikes

Keybind: Q
Cooldown: TBD

Metal Dankles stops in place and slams the ground, creating a line of spiked icicles. The icicles have a solid hitbox and can be used to block off an Experiment's path. Each spike will be destroyed if enough damage is sustained by Metal Dankles himself or Experiments. Fire-based attacks deal more damage to the spikes. The spikes deal a medium amount of damage if they hit an Experiment directly.

If used while flying, Metal Dankles instead drops a spiked ball made of ice that explodes upon hitting the ground. Those caught in the explosion are inflicted with Frostbite.

Relic Burst

Keybind: X
Cooldown: TBD

Metal Dankles releases a large homing projectile, appearing as a musical orb with music notes trailing behind it. The projectile homes in on whichever Experiment is closest to Dankles when the ability is used, and will pass through walls to reach the Experiment. The orb starts out slow but speeds up as it moves, dissipating after a few seconds.
