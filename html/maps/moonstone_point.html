Moonstone Point

Introduction

Home to Prism, one of Inkblot's two major factions, Moonstone Point is a large city with a similarly large wall surrounding it. The city itself is divided into five districts, however only the city's Topaz district is accessible in Shifted in Monochrome.

Appearance

Moonstone Point's Topaz District is the city's main working district, having a park in the center. The district's buildings have varying heights, with some having rooftop terraces and balconies. In Shifted in Monochrome, the map is enshrouded in a dark fog, with a desolate moon hanging low in the sky. Most of the buildings are unable to be entered, however a few, such as Specter's Record Store, can be.

Twilight Park is near the edge of the district. It is a large, grassy area with a few trees, benches, and picnic tables scattered throughout. In the center of it is a fountain, and near the Northwest edge lies a large structure with several tunnels, holes, and walkways.

Mechanics

Once the timer reaches its final 80 seconds, dark inky tendrils and puddles seep out from the city's shadows, slowing Experiments and Experiments alike when they come in contact with the ink. Inkblot however receives a slight speed bonus upon contacting the ink.

The thick tendrils block off certain paths, but open up new ones for those willing to wade through the ink.

Trivia

  • Moonstone Point, as well as Specter themselves, were originally created for a game Gray planned on making called "Shardfall". The concepts evolved significantly since then though, and were a major role in Inkblot being created.
  • Moonstone Point is heavily inspired by Mirror's Edge: Catalyst.
